Fieldwork @ Ödenwinkelkees 2019

Max Hanusch
Oct 15, 2019
Project

The best part of working as an ecologist definitely is fieldwork. Summer 2019 was an amazing three months full of botanizing, arthropod collecting and microbiome sampling. The aim of the project is to better understand the inital processes shaping the development of biodiversity. Along the receding ice fronts of alpine glaciers new ecosystems start to emerge on the sand and soil left behind the glacier snout. For our project, we chose the forefield of the Ödenwinkel glacier as our model system. The Ödenwinkelkees is located at 2100m a.s.l. in the Hohe Tauern National Park, Austria.

As the 2018-19 winter was a record-breaking in terms of snow, most of the surroundings were still covered in snow when we first arrived at the fieldsite by the mid of June.
